Dreamwalker - 3D Game Project


About

This is a part of a project made for an assignment for RMIT University (Bachelor of Digital Media). The brief is to create a response to pain mangament by creating a game or app that can reduce or distract pain. In response to this, I decided to create a game that could help people relax as well as practice concentration.

Game type: 3D first-person adventure game

  • Game name: Dreamwalker
  • Game platform: Playstation VR
  • Game console: Oculus Rift with EEG headset
  • Gameplay: It takes place in a surreal dream-like environment where the player would get a chance to explore and interact with the surrounding using their telekinetic superpower. he superpower is generated by their ability to concentrate which is measured by a headset using Electroencephalography (EEG), a technology that can measure brain waves and detect when the brain is focused or not.


    • Process

    I thought of relating this project with dreams in response to the prompt immersive and presence I get his idea when I encounter a proposal from J. Allan Hobson, a scientist from the Division of Sleep Medicine at Harvard School in which he states that “REM sleep dreaming can be viewed as a virtual reality created from our brain’s devise.” This is because during sleep, the brain is somewhat still active. The prefrontal cortex, the region that handles logic and planning, is less active during REM sleep. Therefore, we frequently don't notice a dream's strangeness or plausibility until we wake up, which is why, until you awaken from that dream, your ability to fly or use a superpower feels so plausible.
